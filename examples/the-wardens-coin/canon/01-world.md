# World Bible

## One-paragraph pitch of the world
> Caldera is a drowned megacity built in vertical tiers inside a flooded crater. The rich live
> in the dry upper rings; the poor in the flooded undercity below. Here, sound carries memory:
> any object can hold the last voice that commanded it, and silence is the most expensive thing
> a person can buy — or be sentenced to.

## Tone & aesthetic
- Visual era / inspiration: drowned art-deco, neon bleeding into black water
- Mood: oppressive, wet, lit from below
- What makes it feel different: objects remember; the deaf cannot be commanded; rain never stops

## Rules of reality (the "physics")
| Rule | What it enables | What it makes impossible |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Sound imprints on matter | objects "remember" the last intent pressed into them | erasing a memory without destroying the object |
| The deaf can't be commanded | Kai is immune to Resonance commands | commanding her back into silence |
| Water muffles resonance | the undercity hides from the Wardenate's listeners | reading a memory once it sinks deep enough |

## Geography & key locations

### LOC-001 — The Spire
- Type: tower (upper city)
- Description: the dry Wardenate seat at the crater's rim; never floods.
- Who controls it: FAC-001 (the Wardenate)
- Travel time: 2 hours up the lift-chains from the market; the undercity can't reach it unseen.

### LOC-002 — The Drowned Market
- Type: tier (mid-city)
- Description: a flooded bazaar of gangways and stalls over black water; neon and rain.
- Who controls it: contested — FAC-002 (the Drowned) run it after dark.
- Travel time: 20 min by skiff down to the lower tiers (LOC-003).

### LOC-003 — The Sealed Archive
- Type: drowned vault (lower undercity)
- Description: a records hall sealed shut during the Sealing; half-submerged, resonance-quiet.
- Who controls it: nobody — sealed by the Warden's own command, which still holds the door.
- Travel time: 20 min up to the market; a half-day's climb to the Spire.

### LOC-004 — Vela's Workshop
- Type: hideaway (lower undercity)
- Description: a tuner's nook of brass forks and dead radios, wedged above the waterline.
- Who controls it: CHR-002 (Vela)
- Travel time: 10 min from the Sealed Archive.

## Factions & power structures

### FAC-001 — The Wardenate
- Goal / ideology: order through silence; "a quiet city is a safe city."
- Leader: CHR-003 (Sael, the Warden)
- Relationship to the hero: her hunter; the author of her deafness (she doesn't know yet).
- Resources / reach: the Spire, the lift-chains, a corps of resonance-listeners.
- Public face vs. true agenda: public — flood control. True — the Sealing was a purge.

### FAC-002 — The Drowned
- Goal / ideology: the undercity's loose mutual-aid network; survival, memory, no masters.
- Leader: none (Vela is a respected elder, not a boss)
- Relationship to the hero: home turf; they tolerate Kai but don't trust outsiders' fights.
- Resources / reach: skiffs, gangway routes, the market after dark.

## Society & culture (only what matters)
- Who has power: those who can be heard — the upper rings, and Resonators.
- Money / economy: silence is currency; a "quiet writ" buys a sound-proofed room or a sealed mouth.
- Law & order: the Wardenate's listeners enforce; sentences are often literal silencings.
- Technology level: early-industrial, lift-chains and brass, no firearms.

## History that the story touches
- The Sealing: the Warden flooded and sealed the three lowest tiers "for flood control." (EVT-001)
- Kai's deafening: she lost her left ear's hearing the night of the Sealing. (EVT-002)

## Open mysteries / unrevealed truths
- Mystery: whose voice is on the coin?  → reveal: the Warden's, named Sael  → ledger: SP-001
- Mystery: what did the archive hold that was worth sealing?  → reveal: the Sealing's order  → ledger: SP-002
