# Premise

## Logline
> A deaf street-thief who can hear objects' memories must expose the Warden who silenced the
> undercity — before the coin carrying his voice leads him straight to the last witness: her.

## Genre & tone
- Primary genre: urban fantasy
- Sub-genre / register: grim seinen with dry, warm humor
- Tone (3 adjectives): rain-soaked, defiant, intimate
- Comparable works: *Dorohedoro*'s grime, *A Silent Voice*'s interiority

## Theme & moral
- Theme (the question): is a voice that no one hears still worth raising?
- Moral / value (the answer): being heard isn't given — it's taken.
- The lie the hero believes at the start: "If no one can hear me, no one can hurt me."
- The truth they learn by the end: silence kept her safe and kept her erased; she chooses neither.

## The hero's want vs. need
- Wants (external/plot): expose the Warden and prove the Sealing was murder.
- Needs (internal/growth): to let herself be heard — to belong instead of merely survive.
- Why want and need conflict: exposing the Warden means being seen, the one thing she avoids.

## Promise to the reader
- This comic promises: an underdog turning her wound into a weapon; a city of secrets that pay off.
- It is NOT: a clean power fantasy. Wins cost her, every time.

## Audience & format
- Target reader: older-teen / adult comics readers
- Format: B&W seinen manga
- Length target: open-ended serial; this bible covers ARC-01
- Content rating: mature (violence, drowning, no explicit content)

## North-star sentence
> She can hear everything except the people she's trying to save.
